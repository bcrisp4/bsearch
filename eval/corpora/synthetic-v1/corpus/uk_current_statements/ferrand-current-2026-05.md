## Understanding the jargon

EAR (Effective Annual Rate) is the cost of an overdraft stated as a yearly rate that takes account of compounding, but not fees. NAR (Nominal Annual Rate) is the simple yearly rate before compounding. AER (Annual Equivalent Rate) shows what a savings rate would be if interest were paid and compounded once each year. Overdraft interest is worked out daily on the amount you are overdrawn and charged monthly.
