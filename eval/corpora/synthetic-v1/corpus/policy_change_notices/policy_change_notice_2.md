# Important information. Changes to your home contents insurance policy.

We review the wording of our home contents insurance policies each year. This notice describes the changes that will apply to your policy from your renewal date on or after 1 July 2022. Please read it alongside your policy booklet and your schedule, and keep it with your policy documents. If anything here is unclear, or if a change affects how you want to be covered, call us on 0113 496 0330.

## What's changing

- We have increased the single-article limit for pedal cycles

kept at home from £350 to £600, and you can now add away-from-home pedal cycle cover as an optional extension - call us before your renewal if you would like a quote.

- We have launched the Alderoak app, where you can view

your policy documents, report a claim and track its progress. Paper documents continue unless you opt out.

- We have increased the limit for personal money kept in the home from £300 to £500, and clarified that gift vouchers and prepaid cards count as personal money.
- We have clarified that damage arising gradually - including wear and tear, rot and gradual water damage -

is not covered. This has always been our position; the new wording makes it explicit.

- We have updated the automatic renewal wording: we will write to you at least 21 days before renewal, and if your premium rises by more than 10% we will ask you to confirm rather than renewing automatically.
- We have added a new "home emergency assist" helpline for all contents customers, giving telephone advice on urgent domestic emergencies. Call-out costs remain covered only where you hold the optional home emergency extension.

Everything else about your policy stays the same. Your renewal invitation, showing your premium and any changes to your personal details or sums insured, will reach you separately around three weeks before your renewal date. If you do not wish to renew, tell us at any time before your renewal date and your cover will simply end - there is no charge for not renewing.
