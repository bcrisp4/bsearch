# Instructions for Employee

## Notice to Employee

Do you have to file? Refer to the federal filing instructions to determine whether you are required to file a tax return. Even if you do not have to file, you should file if you can claim a refundable credit or had income tax withheld from your pay.

**Corrections. If your name, SSN, or address is incorrect, correct Copies B, C, and 2**

and ask Brightpath Payroll for a corrected statement (Form W-2c). Be sure to ask the payroll provider to file the correction with the tax authorities.

## Box 1.

Shows your total taxable wages, tips, and other compensation. Amounts deferred to a 401(k) arrangement (see box 12, code D) are not included in box 1.

## Box 2.

Shows the total federal income tax withheld from your pay during the year. Include this amount on the federal income tax withheld line of your return.

## Boxes 3 and 5.

Show wages subject to social security and Medicare taxes respectively. Elective deferrals under code D remain subject to these taxes, so boxes 3 and 5 may be larger than box 1.

## Box 12 codes (extract).

**D - Elective deferrals to a section 401(k) cash or deferred arrangement. C -**

Taxable cost of group-term life insurance over $50,000. DD - Cost of employersponsored health coverage (not taxable). W - Employer contributions to a health savings account.

## Box 13.

The "Retirement plan" box is checked because you were an active participant in your employer's 401(k) plan; this may limit the deduction for traditional IRA contributions.

## Box 14.

Your employer uses this box to report New York Paid Family Leave contributions, which are after-tax payroll deductions.

## Boxes 15 through 20.

Show state and local wages and income tax withheld. Use these amounts when completing your New York State and New York City returns.
