# The boiler pressure saga

Log of the Calorix C28 Eco losing pressure, because I kept forgetting when
it started.

- **~mid April 2026** — first noticed pressure gauge at 0.6 bar, no hot
  water until I topped it up via the filling loop. Assumed one-off.
- **early May** — dropped again, twice in a fortnight. Topped up to 1.3
  bar each time. No visible leaks under the boiler or on any radiator.
- **late May** — now dropping to below 1 bar every 4-5 days. Booked
  Harwood Heating (NB the boiler people, not the energy supplier — the
  names still get me) and asked them to roll the annual service in.
- **8 June** — engineer (S. Varga this year, not the usual guy) found the
  expansion vessel diaphragm had ruptured. That was the whole problem —
  pressure had nowhere to go when the system heated up, safety valve was
  letting water out, hence the slow drops. Vessel replaced (Calorix part
  EV-18), recharged to 1.0 bar, system re-pressurised, inhibitor re-dosed.
- **10 June** — invoice HH-2306, **£295.08** all in. Service labour £85,
  vessel £96.40 + £45 fitting, inhibitor £19.50, plus VAT. Compare last
  October's plain service at £120. Ouch, but honestly fair.

Pressure has held at 1.3 bar since. Calling this fixed.

The invoice also came with a safety advisory: flue terminal is at the
minimum clearance from the openable window (fine unless the window frame
ever changes, keep it shut when heating runs long) and early scale in the
heat exchanger — they suggest a ~£60 in-line scale reducer at the next
service. CO alarm tested fine.

That advisory is what kicked off [[heat-pump-research]].
