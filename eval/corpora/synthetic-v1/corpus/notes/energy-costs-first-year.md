# Energy — first full year at Redecombe House

Harwood Energy's August bill had a "year in review" panel: **3,043 kWh**
in the twelve months to 31 July 2026. A bit over the 2,450 kWh they
estimated when I moved in, which they blame on the cold January. Seems
right — January was 356 kWh, July only 179.

## Monthly picture (from the bills)

| Bill month | Usage (kWh) | Charges |
|---|---|---|
| Sep 2025 | 197 | £65.77 |
| Nov 2025 | 248 | £78.68 |
| Dec 2025 | 292 | £89.29 |
| Jan 2026 | 341 | £102.21 |
| Feb 2026 | 356 | £111.30 |
| Apr 2026 | 274 | £89.51 |
| Jun 2026 | 204 | £70.92 |
| Aug 2026 | 179 | £64.28 |

(Skipped a few boring middle months. Feb bill covers January usage etc —
their billing lags a month, which confused me for ages.)

Tariff is Fair Deal Flex. Prices went up 1 Jan 2026: unit rate 24.1p →
25.3p, standing charge 48.9p → 51.4p/day. They keep pushing "Fair Deal
Fixed 12" at £748-ish a year in the bill footer; at my usage the flex
tariff cost about the same so I haven't bothered switching.

Direct debit history, for reference: £68 when I joined → cut to £61 from
Oct 2025 → reset up to **£74 from 1 June 2026** after winter ate the
credit. Annoying but the maths on their letter checked out.

Two incidents worth remembering:

1. The standing charge overcharge — they billed 51.2p instead of 48.9p
   over the summer, I flagged it 14 Sep, £2.12 credit appeared on the
   October bill. Full story in [[standing-charge-dispute]].
2. Feb 2026 bill was **estimated** — the smart meter dropped off the
   network on 14 Feb and didn't reconnect until 3 March. April's bill
   trued it up automatically.
