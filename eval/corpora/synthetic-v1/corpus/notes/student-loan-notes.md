# Student loan — state of the sinkhole

Wexcombe 2012-15 vintage. Repayments restarted automatically with the
first proper Halewick payslip: **£54/month** via PAYE since Sep 2025
(threshold's £28,470, they take 9% over it).

The Feb 2026 letter from the Student Finance Agency confirmed the
Sep-Jan payments all landed. Then the June annual statement delivered
the annual comedy:

- Repaid over the year: £378 (7 × £54)
- Interest added: **£2,156.24**
- Balance: £47,850 → **£49,628.24**

Rate went 4.3% → 4.6% from 1 Sep 2025 (they wrote in August to warn).

So the balance grows ~£1,780/yr *after* my repayments. It will never be
repaid; it writes off eventually; it is functionally a 9% marginal tax
with a progress bar moving backwards. Decision, made once, recorded here
so I stop relitigating it: **no voluntary overpayments, ever** — money
beyond the mandatory £54 goes to the emergency fund and the Fernleaf ISA
where it compounds *for* me.

The US years were a repayment pause (below threshold, non-UK employer,
did the overseas income assessment forms) which is partly why the
interest pile is what it is. Worth it. Would pause again.
