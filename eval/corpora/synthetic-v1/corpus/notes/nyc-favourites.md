# NYC list (kept for visitors, and for future nostalgic me)

Two years of "we should write these down". Finally writing these down.
Brooklyn-weighted because that's where I live; Manhattan entries earned
their subway ride.

## Food, the canon

- **Corner Slice Social**, Fort Greene — the upside-down square slice.
  Cash only, closes when the dough runs out, this is not an affectation
  it's a warning
- **Mama Duyên's**, Greenpoint — bún chả, get the extra herbs plate.
  Took everyone who ever visited, 100% conversion rate
- **The Bent Spoon Diner**, near the apartment — nothing special, open
  late, knows my order, the true definition of a local
- **Fiorella's Red Sauce Club**, Carroll Gardens — birthday-tier. The
  sunday gravy thing on the specials board, if it's on, order it, no I
  won't elaborate
- **Halal cart, Vanguard & 6th** (the one with the green umbrella, NOT
  the red one) — the office lunch of champions, Meridian folks know
- **Kossar's-style bialy place on Atlantic** — saturday morning ritual.
  Bialy > bagel, I said what I said, revoke my visa

## Food, honourable mentions

- The dumpling window in Sunset Park that Meridian's design team
  evangelised — worth the D train, order double what feels sane
- Jerk chicken spot on Nostrand, found while lost, returned on purpose
  monthly thereafter
- The diner pancake ranking project (14 diners, two years, a
  spreadsheet): winner was the unfashionable one by the hospital,
  fluffiness is a solved problem there and nowhere else. This research
  cost me nothing but my dignity and roughly $340

## Coffee

- **Lantern & Anchor**, Clinton Hill — where most of this vault got
  written tbh. Flat white completely fine, wifi password unchanged in
  two years, a miracle
- The place under the bridge with no sign — better coffee, no seats,
  different tool for a different job

## Drinks

- **The Cartographer's Rest**, Fort Greene — back garden in summer.
  Where the leaving drinks were, June '25. Got emotional, blaming the
  negronis
- Rooftop at the hotel on Flatbush — overpriced, tourist-adjacent, and
  the sunset over lower Manhattan makes fools of us all. Take visitors

## Not food

- Green-Wood Cemetery > any park for a walk, fight me
- Brooklyn Museum first-saturday nights (free, chaotic, brilliant)
- The Transit Museum is IN a decommissioned subway station and grown
  adults should be more excited about this
- Walk: Brooklyn Bridge at 7am once, then the Manhattan Bridge walkway
  every other time — same view, no crowd, better trains rumbling past
- Prospect Park loop on a borrowed bike, sunday mornings, before the
  crowds and ideally before fully waking up
- The ferry as transport that accidentally happens to be the best-value
  sightseeing in the city. Take it anywhere, the destination is a
  technicality

## Seasonal calibration (hard-won)

- **Summer**: the city is a radiator and everyone pretends otherwise
  until August, when everyone leaves. Copy them or embrace the empty
  city — empty-city August with the museums to yourself was secretly
  top-tier
- **October** is the correct month. All of it. Every visitor should be
  steered here
- **January-February**: the radiator-clank-and-soup era. This is when
  The Bent Spoon earns its place on this list
- Rooftop season officially opens the first 20°C Friday and the whole
  borough knows it simultaneously, book ahead or stand
