# Lake District, 17-19 Oct 2025

First proper UK trip since moving back. Train to Penrith, bus to Keswick,
no car needed (validating the no-car London life extends to holidays).

Stayed: B&B just off the main square, decent breakfast, drying room —
book anywhere with a drying room in October, this is the whole tip.

Walks:
- Sat: Catbells. Short, sharp, rammed, worth it. Views over Derwentwater
  ridiculous even in flat grey light
- Sun: Walla Crag → Ashness Bridge loop before the train. Rain arrived
  horizontal at the top, classic

Kit lessons (writing down so I actually buy things before next time):
- My waterproof is water-resistant. There is a difference. It was
  explained to me, forcefully, by the sky
- Cotton socks, never again

Random: phone reception nonexistent most of Saturday and it was great.
Downloaded maps beforehand — smug about this once, will be smug again.

Next: Buttermere area? Longer route round Haystacks. Take the fancy
camera not the phone.
