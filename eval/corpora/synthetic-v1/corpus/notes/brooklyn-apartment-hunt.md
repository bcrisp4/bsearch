# Apartment hunt — September 2023

Landed with 3 weeks of corporate housing from Meridian and a spreadsheet
brain. NYC rental market as experienced by a fresh arrival: everything is
a scam until proven otherwise, "cozy" means the bed touches the stove,
and the good ones go in 48 hours.

Constraints: ≤ $2,400, under 40 min to the Meridian office near Canal,
laundry in building or on block, no basement "garden level" nonsense.

## The contenders

| # | Where | $/mo | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Bushwick, off the L | 2,150 | great space, the L though. Passed |
| 2 | Crown Heights 1br | 2,300 | lovely, lost it — applied 6 hrs too late |
| 3 | "Clinton Hill" (was not Clinton Hill) | 2,250 | listing fiction, walked out |
| 4 | Fort Greene studio | 2,400 | beautiful, top of budget, 4th floor walkup, moths of doubt |
| 5 | **Calver St apt 3B** | 2,275 | THE ONE — real 1br, actual light, laundry in basement, C train 5 min |

Took #5. Apt 3B, 412 Calver Street. Applied within the hour, from the
coffee shop across the street, on a phone, uploading pay stubs with
shaking thumbs — this is a normal way to secure housing here and
everyone just accepts it. Broker fee hurt in a way I wasn't
emotionally prepared for (a full month, standard here, still barbaric).
Lease signed 22 Sep, keys 1 Oct, guarantor not needed in the end because
the Meridian offer letter did the talking.

First-apartment admin checklist, done over the following fortnight:

- [x] Renters insurance (Umbrella Row, ~$14/mo, required by the lease)
- [x] Bank account — Commonwealth Harbor, the branch on Atlantic. US
  banking is 60% marble lobby, 40% fax machine
- [x] Utilities in my name (electric only, heat included — radiators
  controlled by a valve gremlin, learned to open windows in January like
  a local)
- [x] Internet — one (1) provider serves the building. Monopoly pricing,
  $65 for "up to" speeds. UK me from 2026 pays £34.99 for symmetric
  fibre and thinks of this monthly

## Field notes from the viewings (kept verbatim, they deserve it)

- Viewing 1: "the bedroom fits a queen" — it fit a queen the way a
  glovebox fits a glove, diagonally and with commitment
- Viewing 3: listing said Clinton Hill, the walk from the actual subway
  stop said otherwise by 25 minutes. Broker's counter-argument: "it's
  all one neighborhood really". It is not
- A viewing not even numbered above: the shower was IN the kitchen.
  Not near. In. Priced accordingly, which is to say only $100 less
- The Fort Greene studio (#4): I still think about that light. Fourth
  floor southern exposure is a controlled substance
- Calver St viewing: the current tenant was home, said "the radiator
  bangs in winter but the super actually answers" — the single most
  valuable data point of the entire search, no listing will ever tell
  you this, always talk to the outgoing tenant

What I'd tell someone doing this: bring every document you own to every
viewing, decide your walk-away number before the broker starts talking,
and the apartment you get beats the apartment that got away once your
books are on shelves. #2 still stings though. (2026 postscript, filed
from London: the radiator did bang, the super did answer, tenant lady
was right about everything. I hope apt 3B's next person got the same
briefing from me — I did my best on the handover walk-through.)
