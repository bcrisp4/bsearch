# Sinus stuff — the long arc

Keeping the thread in one place because it now spans two countries and
four years of paperwork.

## The story so far

- **Forever**: left nostril mostly decorative, esp at night. 3-4 sinus
  infections/yr. Childhood football injury the likely culprit per the
  NYC specialist
- **Oct 2024, NYC** — finally saw an ENT (Riverside, referred by my PCP).
  Verdict: septum deviated left with a spur, turbinates swollen.
  Endoscopy done in-office (unpleasant, brief). Plan: steroid spray
  (fluticasone, 2 sprays each side daily) + big saline rinses, 8 weeks
  minimum before judging. Surgery (septoplasty) on the table if meds fail
- **Jan 2025, NYC follow-up** — it worked, mostly. Left side opens most
  nights, snoring down (partner-verified), one mild infection over the
  holidays that cleared solo. Chose to keep managing with the spray
  rather than surgery. Got told the anatomy won't change, symptom control
  is the game. Records exported via the portal before the move ✓
- **Oct 2025, London** — registered at Calverley Park, new-patient bloods
  on the 13th. All normal except vitamin D "borderline low" (42, they
  like >50) → cheap daily supplement Oct-Mar now. The classic UK welcome
- **Winter 25/26** — two infections (Nov, early Jan). Trajectory going
  the wrong way vs the NYC years. The Jan one took antibiotics
- **14 Jan 2026** — Dr Ravensworth referred me onward to ENT: Mr
  Penhaligon at Southmere Park. She'd read the US notes I brought,
  which made the whole conversation 10x better. BRING RECORDS, always
- **10 Mar 2026** — appointment, ENT outpatients clinic 4B, 10:40.
  Endoscopy round two. His take: same anatomy, medical management has
  gone from "working" to "mostly working", reasonable to now discuss
  septoplasty properly. On the routine list for a surgical planning
  conversation; letter to follow. Bring the snoring complaints in
  writing, he said, deadpan

## Comparing the two systems, since I'm rare enough to have used both

- US: PCP referral → ENT seen in 12 days, endoscopy same visit,
  itemised bill arrived later like a jump scare (insurance ate most of
  it, the EOB paperwork was its own part-time job)
- UK: GP referral 14 Jan → seen 10 Mar, ~8 weeks, zero paperwork, zero
  cost at point of use, now an open-ended wait for the surgical slot
- Net: the US bought speed with admin and money; the UK buys simplicity
  with queues. For a chronic non-urgent thing like a wonky septum, the
  queue is honestly fine — the infections are annoying, not dangerous
- Both countries' specialists gave the same diagnosis from the same
  nostril, which is reassuring about medicine as a discipline

## Current regime

- fluticasone 2 sprays/side, morning, ongoing (repeat via Calverley Park)
- saline rinse evenings during winter; lapsed in summer, restart Oct
- vit D Oct-Mar
- decongestant spray: banned substance since 2024, rebound is real and
  the NYC doc's lecture about it still rings in both (partially
  functional) nostrils
