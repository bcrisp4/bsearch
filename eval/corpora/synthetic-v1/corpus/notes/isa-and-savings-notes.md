# Savings map, new tax year edition

Post-US simplification finally done. Where everything lives and why:

| Pot | Where | For | Feeds |
|---|---|---|---|
| Current | Ferrand current acct | bills + monthly float | salary in |
| Emergency fund | Ferrand savings | 3 months of fixed costs, target ~£7k | £150/mo + bonuses |
| S&S ISA | Fernleaf Invest | the long game, global index, don't touch, don't look | £300/mo, 1st |
| US leftovers | Commonwealth Harbor | ~$800 remnant + whatever interest | nothing, wind down |

Notes to self:

- Fernleaf ISA: £300/mo standing order started Sep 2025. Set-and-forget
  is the entire strategy; the one time I logged in during the spring
  wobble I nearly did something clever. Don't be clever
- Ferrand savings interest counts against the personal savings
  allowance — the annual interest certificate covers it, and this year
  (first full UK year) it's nowhere near the £1,000. Non-issue until the
  emergency fund fattens up
- The US accounts generate those withholding tax forms even on trivial
  interest — two of them arrived for last year, one per account, near
  identical, filing confusion guaranteed. Close the savings side next
  time I'm dealing with Commonwealth Harbor anyway; keep checking alive
  until the final tax year wraps
- ISA allowance used last year: £2,100 of £20k. The allowance is not a
  target, Theo, the emergency fund comes first. (It does sting slightly)

Rule reaffirmed after reading too many forum threads: no individual
stocks in the ISA. The Kestrel of my finances is a boring global index
and the solver converges on "keep buying it".
