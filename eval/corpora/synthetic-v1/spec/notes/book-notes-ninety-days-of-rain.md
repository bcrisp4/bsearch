# Ninety Days of Rain — notes (no spoilers version for lending it out)

Novel of the year so far (March, but confident). Estuary town, three
narrators, one flood season, and the water level doing the pacing the
whole way through — chapter headings are just river gauge readings,
which sounds gimmicky and is instead quietly devastating.

Why it worked on me specifically:

- The middle narrator is an engineer whose competence is written
  *correctly* — she's good at her job in ways the plot respects.
  Vanishingly rare
- Weather as antagonist without being a disaster novel
- The ending withholds exactly the right amount. Rosh disagrees,
  strongly, at length, over two dinners now

Lent to: Priti (returned, converted), Mum (pending since Easter).

Pairs weirdly well with The Quiet Grid — infrastructure as the thing
love stories happen on top of.
