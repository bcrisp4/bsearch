# Heat pump noise — research for the quote question

Open question from [[heat-pump-research]]: the monobloc unit goes on an
external wall bracket, and I share that wall's airspace with the
neighbours' bedroom window one floor up. How loud is loud?

What I found:

- Modern monoblocs quote ~55-60 dB(A) sound *power*; what matters is
  sound *pressure* at the neighbour's window, which drops with distance
  and barriers. Rule-of-thumb calcs in the installer guidance put a
  compliant install well under night-time background for a London street
- There's a standard permitted-development noise assessment installers
  must do — 42 dB(A) at the nearest neighbouring window is the magic
  number. Aurellon's survey mentioned doing this calc; I can't find any
  noise line on the Fenwick & Dray paperwork. Add to the questions list ✓
- Anti-vibration mounts matter more than the spec sheet — bracket
  transmission through the wall is what generates complaints, not air
  noise. Aurellon's quote has a £310 line for bracket + anti-vibration
  kit, which reads better every time I learn something new
- Defrost cycles in winter are the loudest moments (brief). Position
  matters: not directly under an openable window if avoidable. Ours
  would be… directly under ours, not theirs. Acceptable selfishness

Conclusion for the landlord doc: noise is a solved problem IF the
assessment is done and vibration isolation is fitted — which is an
argument for the more thorough quote, and I'm starting to sound like an
Aurellon salesman, which at £2,460 extra of Wrenmoor's money I suppose
I can afford to be.
