# Dal (the £1.20-a-portion winter engine)

Adapted from three internet versions until it stopped being anyone
else's. Doubles fine, freezes perfectly, made weekly Nov-Mar.

1. Onion diced, fried properly soft in ghee/oil — 10 min, don't rush,
   the dal knows
2. Garlic, ginger, 1 green chilli. 2 min
3. Spices: tsp each turmeric + cumin, half tsp mustard seeds till they
   pop
4. 250g red lentils, rinsed. Tin of tomatoes. 750ml stock. Simmer ~25
   min, it's done when it collapses
5. Salt at the END (lentils stay tough otherwise, learned this in
   Brooklyn from Meridian-era flatmate, the one lasting technical
   handover from that apartment)
6. Tarka on top if showing off: butter, more cumin seeds, chilli flakes,
   curry leaves when the freezer stash allows

Serve: rice or just yoghurt + naan from the shop downstairs. Lime.

Freezer note: flat bags, label them — the great "dal or bolognese?"
lottery of January was funny exactly once.
