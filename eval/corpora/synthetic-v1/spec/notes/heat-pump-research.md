# Heat pump — should the flat ditch the gas combi?

Started this after the June boiler service. The engineer replaced the
expansion vessel (see [[boiler-pressure-saga]]) and left a safety advisory
about the flue terminal sitting at the *minimum* permitted clearance from
the bedroom window. Boiler is a 2021 Calorix C28 Eco, so approaching five
years, and the advisory also flagged early scale on the heat exchanger.
None of it is urgent but it got me thinking: if this thing needs replacing
in 2-3 years anyway, why fit another gas boiler?

Emailed Sophie at Penfold & Reach, who passed it to the landlord. Slightly
to my surprise Wrenmoor came back with "get us two quotes and we'll look at
it seriously" — I suspect the £7,500 grant plus the EPC pressure on
landlords is doing the persuading, not me.

## The two quotes

Surveys were 16 and 24 June, quotes landed 22 and 29 June.

| | Aurellon Renewables | Fenwick & Dray |
|---|---|---|
| Unit | Caldera Aero 6 monobloc | Thermik T60, 6 kW |
| Cylinder | Caldera 180 L slimline | 170 L unvented |
| Heat loss calc | 4.9 kW, room-by-room | 5.1 kW |
| Radiators upsized | 3 | 2 |
| Quote total | **£11,940** | **£9,480** |
| After £7,500 grant | £4,440 | £1,980 |

So Fenwick & Dray are £2,460 cheaper. Where the gap comes from, as far as
I can tell from the line items:

- Aurellon's unit itself is dearer (£5,240 vs £3,890 for the Thermik) and
  their cylinder is £1,380 vs £1,120.
- Aurellon do a third radiator and throw in a magnetic filter with the
  system flush; F&D is a chemical flush only.
- Aurellon include commissioning + handover + **first-year monitoring**
  (£350). F&D just do commissioning and the building-control notification.
- F&D include removing and disposing of the old boiler (£250) — I actually
  can't see boiler disposal called out on the Aurellon quote at all, need
  to ask whether it's buried in the labour line.
- Aurellon credit the £150 survey fee back if you accept.

## Quality-of-quote gut feel

Aurellon's paperwork is just better. Room-by-room heat loss (4.9 kW),
design output stated as 5.8 kW at −2°C, SCOP of 4.1, and an actual running
cost comparison: **£742/yr on gas vs £618/yr with the heat pump** at
current prices, plus ~1.1 tonnes CO2 saved. Their designer (Maya
Brenchley) walked me round the numbers on the phone for half an hour.

Fenwick & Dray felt more "boiler firm that also fits heat pumps". Surveyor
was in and out in 40 minutes, whole-house heat loss figure, no running
cost estimate, no SCOP stated. Cheaper for a reason? Or just less
paperwork? Genuinely unsure.

Sanity check against [[energy-costs-first-year]]: we used 3,043 kWh of
electricity in yr 1. Aurellon's gas-vs-HP running figures are for the
*heating*, separate from that. Their £618/yr estimate at 25.3p/kWh implies
~2,450 kWh/yr of heat pump usage, which would roughly double our
electricity bill while killing the gas bill and the gas standing charge
entirely. Standing charge alone is 51.4p/day = £188/yr, so the "dual fuel
standing charge" saving is real money.

Aurellon — the design work and monitoring feel worth it. But it's
Wrenmoor's money and I'd bet rent they pick the cheap one.
