# Moving back — how it actually went

Two years in Brooklyn, done. Landed 12 July, lived out of a bag at Mum's
in Ottersley for three weeks, keys to the flat on **1 August**. Writing
this down while I still remember the order things had to happen in,
because the dependency graph of UK admin is genuinely worse than anything
at work.

## The flat

Flat 12, Redecombe House — found through Penfold & Reach. Agent was
Sophie Trelawny, who was actually responsive, a first for the industry.
Landlord is a company (Wrenmoor Estates) not a person, which so far means
repairs get approved by email in days rather than never.

- Tenancy: 12 months from 1 Aug 2025, ref PR/RED/1207
- Deposit: **£1,850**, protected on 8 Aug — certificate in the files.
  Chased Sophie for the certificate on day 6 because the scheme says 30
  days but I've been burned before (Millbeck Road, 2015, never again)
- Rent £1,600 pcm, which after Brooklyn feels almost reasonable. Almost.

## The circular dependency problem

You need a UK address for a bank account, a bank account for the tenancy
direct debit, and ideally a tenancy for the address. Two years away nearly
reset everything. Order that worked:

1. Signed tenancy using Mum's address + US bank statements as proof of
   funds (Penfold & Reach were fine with Commonwealth Harbor statements,
   translated the balance at that day's rate)
2. Ferrand Bank account opened 1 Aug, the day the tenancy started, using
   the signed tenancy agreement as address proof. Confirmation letter
   arrived on the 4th
3. Everything else hangs off Ferrand: council tax DD, Harwood Energy,
   Loopfibre (installed 18 Aug), Brackenwater eventually found me on
   their own, spooky

## Money plumbing between two countries

Worth recording because I'll forget and someone will ask: moved the bulk
of the US savings over in two chunks via a currency broker, not the
banks — the spread difference paid for a month's rent, not exaggerating.
Left ~$800 in Commonwealth Harbor checking to keep it alive for any
final-tax-year fallout and refunds; their savings account is now empty
and should get closed properly. Kept the US phone number alive on a $5
plan for six months purely for bank 2FA texts, which turned out to be
the single smartest admin decision of the whole move. Every US
institution assumes you have a US number forever. Plan around it.

The last Brightpath paystub and the partial-year W-2 chased me across
the ocean anyway despite updating the address in June — mail forwarding
caught them. Tax for the split year is its own saga (Gordon the
accountant is dealing with the US side, worth every penny of his fee,
which is a sentence I never expected to write about an accountant).

## What I'd tell past me

Ship less. Half the boxes from Brooklyn were books I could've sold and
rebought. The shipping quote was more than the sofa cost new. Two
suitcases and a laptop bag covered the first six weeks completely fine
— the boxes didn't even arrive until late September and by then I'd
stopped missing anything in them, which tells you everything.

Keep every letter from the first three months. Between the bank, the
council, and the deposit scheme I've been asked for "proof of address
dated within 3 months" five separate times, and each org wants a
different flavour.
