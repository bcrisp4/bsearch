Disability Income Details

Total Disability means that, solely because of Injury or Sickness, You are unable to perform the material and substantial duties of Your Occupation, and You are not working in any other occupation. We will treat You as Totally Disabled if You have a loss of the use of two limbs, or total loss of sight, speech or hearing, even if You are able to work.

Residual Disability means that, solely because of Injury or Sickness, You are working in Your Occupation or another occupation but Your Income is reduced by at least 20% of Prior Income. A Residual Disability benefit is payable after the Elimination Period in proportion to the percentage of Prior Income lost; where the loss exceeds 75%, We pay the full Monthly Benefit Amount.

Your Occupation means the occupation, or occupations if more than one, in which You are regularly engaged at the time Disability begins. If You are working in a professional or technical specialty, Your Occupation means that specialty.

Successive Periods of Disability resulting from the same or a related cause are treated as one continuous period unless separated by Your return to full-time work for at least 6 consecutive months.

Waiver of Premium. After You have been Disabled for 90 consecutive days, We will waive premiums that become due while Disability continues, and refund premiums paid for that 90-day period. Premium payments resume on the first premium due date after Disability ends.

Rehabilitation Benefit. If You participate in a vocational rehabilitation program approved by Us in writing, We will pay the reasonable costs of the program not covered by other insurance or public funding, in addition to any other benefit payable.

Presumptive Recovery. Benefits end on the earliest of the date You are no longer Disabled, the end of the Maximum Period of Payment, and the date You fail to provide required proof of continuing Disability.

Exclusions and Limitations

1. We will not pay benefits for a Disability caused by, contributed to by, or resulting from war or an act of war, declared or undeclared, or Your active participation in a riot.

2. We will not pay benefits for a Disability caused by an intentionally self-inflicted injury.

3. We will not pay benefits for any period of Disability during which You are incarcerated.

4. We will not pay benefits for a Disability caused by Your commission of, or attempt to commit, a felony, or Your engagement in an illegal occupation.

5. We will not pay benefits for a Disability resulting from cosmetic surgery, except that reconstructive surgery following a covered Sickness or Injury, or surgery to donate an organ, is treated as a Sickness.

6. Benefits for a Disability due to Mental Disorder or Substance Abuse are limited to a combined lifetime maximum of 24 months of benefit payments, except while You are confined in a hospital or institution licensed to treat the condition.

7. The Pre-Existing Condition Limitation on the policy face page applies to conditions, whether diagnosed or undiagnosed, for which You received advice or treatment, or experienced symptoms that would cause an ordinarily prudent person to seek advice or treatment, within 24 months before the Coverage Effective Date.

8. Benefits are not payable for any period of Disability during which You are not under the Regular Care of a Physician appropriate for the disabling condition, unless We agree in writing that continued care would be of no benefit.

Other Features

Transplant and Cosmetic Surgery. If You are Disabled because You donate an organ, or because of complications of pregnancy, the Disability is treated as a Sickness under this policy.

Concurrent Disability. A Disability caused by more than one Injury or Sickness, or by both, is treated as one Disability; benefits are not doubled.

Suspension During Unemployment. If You are unemployed for at least 60 consecutive days, You may suspend this policy for up to 12 months. No premiums are due and no benefits are payable during suspension; coverage resumes without evidence of insurability when premium payments restart.

End of Coverage

This policy ends on the earliest of: the date You ask Us in writing to end it; the Guarantee Date, if You do not renew; the end of the grace period after an unpaid premium; and Your death. Ending of the policy does not affect a claim for a Disability that begins while the policy is in force.

Premium and Reinstatement

Grace Period. A grace period of 31 days is allowed for each premium after the first. The policy stays in force during the grace period. If the premium is not paid by the end of the grace period, the policy lapses as of the due date.

Reinstatement. If the policy lapses, it may be reinstated within one year on written application and payment of all overdue premiums with interest at 6% per year. We may require evidence of insurability. A reinstated policy covers only Sickness that first appears more than 10 days after reinstatement, and Injury sustained after reinstatement.

Premium Refund at Death. We will refund the part of any premium paid for a period beyond the date of Your death.

Claim Provisions

Notice of Claim. Written notice of claim must be given to Us within 30 days after Disability begins, or as soon as reasonably possible. Notice given by or for You to Our head office, or to Our authorized agent, with information sufficient to identify You, is notice to Us.

Claim Forms. When We receive notice of claim, We will send claim forms within 15 days. If We do not, You may meet the proof of loss requirement by giving Us a written statement of the nature and extent of the loss within the time limit below.

Proof of Loss. Written proof of loss must be given to Us within 90 days after the end of each monthly period for which benefits are claimed. Failure to furnish proof within that time will not reduce a claim if it was not reasonably possible to give proof on time and proof is given as soon as reasonably possible, and in any event within one year unless You lack legal capacity.

Time of Payment of Claims. Accrued benefits will be paid monthly during a period of Disability. Any balance remaining unpaid at the end of the period of liability will be paid immediately on receipt of due written proof.

Physical Examination. We may have You examined, at Our expense, as often as reasonably necessary while a claim is pending or benefits are being paid, and may require documentation of Income for Residual Disability claims, including tax returns and payroll records.

Legal Action. No legal action may be brought to recover on this policy within 60 days after written proof of loss has been given, nor after 3 years from the time written proof of loss is required.

General Provisions

Entire Contract; Changes. This policy, with the application and any attached riders, endorsements and amendments, is the entire contract. No change is valid unless approved in writing by one of Our officers and noted on or attached to this policy. No agent may change the policy or waive any of its provisions.

Time Limit on Certain Defenses (Incontestability). After this policy has been in force for 2 years during Your lifetime, excluding any period during which You are Disabled, We cannot contest the statements in the application. No claim for loss incurred or Disability that begins after 2 years from the Coverage Effective Date will be reduced or denied on the ground that a condition not excluded by name or specific description had existed before the Coverage Effective Date.

Misstatement of Age. If Your age has been misstated, the benefits will be those the premium paid would have purchased at the correct age.

Conformity with State Statutes. Any provision of this policy which, on the Coverage Effective Date, conflicts with the statutes of the state in which You reside on that date is amended to conform to the minimum requirements of those statutes.

Assignment. No assignment of this policy is binding on Us unless it is in writing and received at Our head office. We are not responsible for the validity of any assignment.

Glossary

Income means Your gross earned income from salary, wages, commissions, bonuses and fees, less normal business expenses, before taxes. It does not include unearned income such as dividends, interest or rent.

Injury means accidental bodily injury sustained while this policy is in force.

Mental Disorder means a condition classified as a mental disorder in the current edition of the standard diagnostic manual of mental disorders, regardless of cause.

Physician means a licensed medical practitioner acting within the scope of that license, other than You or a member of Your immediate family.

Prior Income means the greater of Your average monthly Income for the 12 months, and for the highest-earning 24 consecutive months in the 60 months, immediately before Disability begins.

Sickness means illness or disease which first manifests itself while this policy is in force.

Substance Abuse means abuse of, or psychological or physical dependence on, alcohol or drugs, other than medication taken as prescribed by a Physician.
