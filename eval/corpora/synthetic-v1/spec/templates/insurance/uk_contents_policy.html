Summary of what is not covered

The full list is in your policy booklet. The main things we do not cover are: damage arising gradually, including wear and tear, rot and gradual water damage; the escape-of-water excess shown in this schedule; theft from the home unless there is evidence of forced or violent entry, where the home is left unoccupied for more than 30 consecutive days; breakage of electronic items unless accidental damage cover has been added; money beyond the limit shown; motor vehicles and their accessories; and loss where any part of a claim is deliberately exaggerated or supported by false documents, in which case the whole claim is forfeit and the policy may be cancelled from the date of the fraud.

Making a claim

Call our 24-hour claims line on 0113 496 0344 or claim in the Alderoak app. Have your policy number ready, tell the police first about any theft, vandalism or loss away from the home and keep the crime reference, and do not dispose of damaged items until we have agreed your claim. Index-linking, where shown in your endorsements, adjusts your sum insured automatically — you do not need to tell us about ordinary replacement purchases.

Important information

Cancellation. You may cancel within 14 days of the start date or of receiving your documents, whichever is later, for a full refund provided you have not claimed. After that, you may cancel at any time and we refund the premium for the remaining full months, less a £15 administration charge, provided no claim has been made in the current period of insurance.

Complaints. Talk to us first: most concerns are resolved in one call. If you remain unhappy eight weeks after raising a complaint, or we issue a final response you do not accept, you may refer the matter to the statutory ombudsman service for financial services, free of charge, within six months of our final response.

Compensation. Alderoak is covered by the statutory compensation scheme for financial services. You may be entitled to compensation from the scheme if we cannot meet our obligations; general insurance claims are covered for 90% of the claim with no upper limit.

Renewal. We write to you at least 21 days before each renewal date with your new premium. If your premium rises by more than 10% we ask you to confirm rather than renewing automatically. Last year’s premium is shown alongside the new premium in your renewal invitation.

Accessibility. We can provide your documents in braille, large print or audio — call 0113 496 0212 or ask in the app and we will set this up for all future documents.

Law and language. This policy is governed by the law of England and Wales and all communication will be in English.
