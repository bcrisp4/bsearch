About your charges

Your home has a water meter, so you pay for what you use: a charge per cubic metre (1 m³ = 1,000 litres) for the clean water we supply and for the wastewater we take away and treat, plus a fixed daily charge for each service that covers meter reading, billing and maintaining the network. Charges are set each April for the charging year.

Help when you need it

If you're finding bills hard to manage, our reduced-rate support tariff can cap charges for eligible households, and our payment-plan team can spread arrears without fees — call 0113 496 0208. If you or someone in your home relies on water for medical reasons, join our priority services register for free extra support during supply interruptions.

If you have a complaint, contact us first and we'll aim to put things right within ten working days. If you're not happy with our final answer you can take your complaint, free of charge, to the independent consumer body for the water sector.

Leaks. We repair leaks on our pipes; the pipe between the boundary and your home is the property's responsibility. If a hidden leak inflates your bill you may qualify for a one-off leak allowance — ask us.

Water quality. Your supply is tested throughout the year. For the latest quality report for your postcode visit brackenwater.example/quality.

Supply interruptions. Planned work is notified in advance by card; live updates during incidents are at brackenwater.example/updates.
