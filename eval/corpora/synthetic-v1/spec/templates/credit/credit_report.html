Understanding Your Score

The factors below have the largest effect on your score this month. Factors marked “helping” are raising your score; those marked “holding back” are lowering it.

HelpingNo missed or late payments reported in the last six years on any open account.
HelpingElectoral roll registration confirmed at your present address.
HelpingLow use of available credit — your reported card balances are a small proportion of your combined credit limits.
Holding backSeveral accounts on your report were opened recently, which lowers your average account age.
Holding backA change of address in the last twelve months. Lenders see stability build over time at one address.

Your score is calculated only from the data in this report. It is refreshed each time data providers report, usually monthly, and whenever you request a report. Lenders do not see this score; they see the underlying data and apply their own scoring.

How To Read This Report

Each data item appears in a box with a black index chip (C1, C2… for credit accounts, P1… for searches, R1… for rental data, A1… for linked addresses). Empty sections say so explicitly — an empty section is good news more often than not. Monthly grids read left to right from the most recent month; the balance row shows the balance reported at each month end, and for revolving accounts the payment rows show what was paid against the previous statement. If anything in this report looks wrong, use the “Query this entry” link in your online report or write to Consumer Services at the address in the Useful Addresses section — disputes are answered within 28 days, and disputed entries are marked as disputed while the data supplier investigates.
